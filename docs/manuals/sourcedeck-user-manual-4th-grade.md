# SourceDeck User Manual

This is a guide written in plain English. You do not need to know computers very well to read it. We use short sentences. We use simple words.

If a word seems hard, we will explain it.

---

## What SourceDeck does

SourceDeck is an app you run on your Mac. It helps you do government contract work.

Government contract work means selling things or services to the United States government. People call this **GovCon** for short.

SourceDeck helps you:

- Find new chances to bid on a contract.
- Read the contract papers and pull out the rules.
- Make notes and plans.
- Track your work from the start to the end.
- Make an internal draft you can read with your team before you send anything.

**SourceDeck does not send anything for you.** You always do the sending and the submitting on your own. SourceDeck just helps you get ready.

---

## What you need to know first

- SourceDeck does not send emails.
- SourceDeck does not submit bids.
- SourceDeck does not upload files to SAM.gov, PIEE, eBuy, GSA, or any portal.
- SourceDeck does not promise that you will win a contract.
- SourceDeck does not check if your paperwork is legal. You and your team must check that.

You are always the boss. You decide what to send and when to send it.

## Tabs in SourceDeck

You will see tabs on the left side. Each tab is one part of the app. Here is what each tab does.

### Command Center

This is your daily home base. It shows you what is happening today. You can see your leads, your tasks, and any alerts.

### Leads

A **lead** is someone who might become a customer. The Leads tab is where you keep a list of leads. You can add a lead by hand. You can also let an AI helper find leads for you.

### GovCon

The GovCon tab is for finding government contracts. You can search SAM.gov. You can save a contract you want to bid on. You can read the contract here and pull out the rules.

### Proposal Workspace

A **proposal** is the long paper you send when you want to bid on a government contract. The Proposal Workspace helps you write the proposal one part at a time.

You write each section by yourself. The AI can help with one section at a time. You always say yes or no to each draft.

There is no button to write the whole proposal at once. That is on purpose. A proposal you do not read is a proposal you will not win.

### Pipeline

The Pipeline tab shows you all your deals. You can see which deals are early and which are close to done. You can also see how much money each deal might bring in.

### Pilot Tracker (or Client Delivery)

This tab helps you track your test run of SourceDeck. It shows you which day of the test you are on. It shows you any problems you wrote down. It shows you if you are ready to go on with paid use.

### Reports

The Reports tab makes a one-page summary of your work. You can show this to your team or your boss.

### Settings

This is where you set up SourceDeck. You add your SAM.gov key here. You add your AI key here. You change the way the app looks here.

### Help / FAQ

The Help tab has answers to common questions. You can read it any time.

## Common rules

A few rules to remember:

- Always read what SourceDeck makes before you use it.
- Always check the facts. The AI can make mistakes.
- Never share your API keys with anyone. Not in a chat. Not in a picture. Not in an email.
- The SAM search screen will not ask you for your SAM key. You only enter your key in two places: the Setup Wizard or in Settings. If you see a SAM key box on the SAM search page, stop and tell your support person.

## FAQ — GovCon

**Q1. What is the GovCon tab?**
A. GovCon is where you find and track United States government contracts.

**Q2. Why is the SAM.gov key needed?**
A. The SAM.gov key lets SourceDeck search the SAM.gov site for contracts.

**Q3. Where do I put the SAM.gov key?**
A. Put the key in the Setup Wizard, or in Settings, in the API Keys area.

**Q4. Can I paste my SAM.gov key on the SAM search page?**
A. No. The SAM search page does not ask for a key. If you see a key box there, stop and tell your operator.

**Q5. What is a pursuit?**
A. A pursuit is a contract you want to bid on.

**Q6. How do I start a pursuit?**
A. Find a contract in SAM, click Save, and add it to GovCon as a pursuit.

**Q7. What is a solicitation?**
A. A solicitation is the official paper the government sends out asking for bids.

**Q8. What is FAR?**
A. FAR stands for Federal Acquisition Regulation. It is the big rule book.

**Q9. What is a clause?**
A. A clause is one rule inside the FAR.

**Q10. What is a Compliance Matrix?**
A. The Compliance Matrix is a chart that shows you matched each rule with a part of your proposal.

**Q11. What does Section L mean?**
A. Section L is the part of the solicitation that says how to write your proposal.

**Q12. What does Section M mean?**
A. Section M is the part that says how your proposal will be graded.

**Q13. What is a set-aside?**
A. A set-aside is when the government saves a contract for one type of small business.

**Q14. What is SDVOSB?**
A. SDVOSB means Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business.

**Q15. What is HUBZone?**
A. HUBZone is a kind of small business in a place with low income.

**Q16. What is 8(a)?**
A. 8(a) is a special program for small businesses.

**Q17. What is WOSB?**
A. WOSB means Woman-Owned Small Business.

**Q18. Can SourceDeck submit a bid for me?**
A. No. You always submit the bid yourself, outside SourceDeck.

**Q19. Can SourceDeck make me win?**
A. No. SourceDeck helps you get ready. It does not promise wins.

**Q20. Where do I go after I find a pursuit?**
A. Move to Proposal Workspace to start drafting your sections.

## FAQ — Proposal Workspace

**Q1. What is the Proposal Workspace?**
A. The Proposal Workspace helps you write a proposal one part at a time.

**Q2. Can I write the whole proposal in one click?**
A. No. That is on purpose. You write each section one by one.

**Q3. Why not all at once?**
A. A proposal you do not read is a proposal you will not win. We slow you down so you can think.

**Q4. How many sections are there?**
A. There are 13 sections.

**Q5. What are the 13 sections?**
A. Table of Contents, Solicitation Summary, Compliance Matrix, Technical Approach, Management Approach, Staffing, Past Performance, Quality Control, Risk Management, Transition, Cost/Price, Attachments, and Final Review.

**Q6. What is the status of a section?**
A. The status tells you where the section is. Not Started, Drafted, Approved, Needs Revision, or User Finalized.

**Q7. What does Drafted mean?**
A. Drafted means you have something written but you have not said it is good yet.

**Q8. What does Approved mean?**
A. Approved means you read it and you like it.

**Q9. What does Needs Revision mean?**
A. Needs Revision means it needs work. The AI can try again.

**Q10. What does User Finalized mean?**
A. User Finalized means you are done with this section. It is locked.

**Q11. Can I unlock a section?**
A. Yes. Click Reset Section to open it again.

**Q12. What is the Notes box for?**
A. The Notes box is for your own thoughts on the section. It also helps the AI write better.

**Q13. Can the AI help me write?**
A. Yes, if you add an AI key in Settings. The AI helps with one section at a time.

**Q14. Can the AI write the whole proposal?**
A. No. The AI only writes one section per click.

**Q15. Do I have to use the AI?**
A. No. You can type your own draft.

**Q16. How do I export my proposal?**
A. Click "Export Internal Review". It saves a local file on your Mac.

**Q17. Is the export an official proposal?**
A. No. It is an internal review draft. You take it to your team.

**Q18. Does SourceDeck send the export anywhere?**
A. No. It saves to your Mac only.

**Q19. Can SourceDeck submit my proposal?**
A. No. You submit outside SourceDeck.

**Q20. What is the Final Internal Review section for?**
A. It is your last check before you take the package out of SourceDeck.

## FAQ — Pipeline

**Q1. What is the Pipeline?**
A. The Pipeline shows all your deals from start to end.

**Q2. What is a deal?**
A. A deal is a chance to win a contract.

**Q3. What is a stage?**
A. A stage is one step in the deal. Like New, Working, Submitted, or Won.

**Q4. How do I move a deal forward?**
A. Click the deal and change the stage.

**Q5. Can SourceDeck submit a deal for me?**
A. No. You mark "Submitted outside SourceDeck" when you have submitted on your own.

**Q6. What does Expected Value mean?**
A. Expected Value is the dollar amount you think the deal is worth.

**Q7. How is the forecast made?**
A. The forecast adds up the values of deals by stage.

**Q8. Is the forecast a promise?**
A. No. It is just an estimate. Real wins are not promised.

**Q9. What is the Won stage?**
A. Won means you won the contract.

**Q10. What is the Lost stage?**
A. Lost means you did not win.

**Q11. Can a deal be both won and lost?**
A. No. A deal is one or the other.

**Q12. Can I see my Pipeline in a chart?**
A. Yes. Open Reports to see it as a chart.

**Q13. Can I delete a deal?**
A. Yes. Click the deal and pick Delete.

**Q14. Can I add a deal by hand?**
A. Yes. Use the Add Deal button.

**Q15. Does my Pipeline share with my team?**
A. No. The data lives on your Mac.

**Q16. Where do leads turn into deals?**
A. When you mark a lead "Qualified", you can move it to Pipeline as a deal.

**Q17. Does Pipeline talk to GovCon?**
A. Yes. A pursuit in GovCon can become a deal in Pipeline.

**Q18. Can I track my follow-up date?**
A. Yes. Each deal has a Next Follow-Up date.

**Q19. Does SourceDeck remind me?**
A. SourceDeck shows the date. Your own calendar or alarm reminds you.

**Q20. Can I add notes to a deal?**
A. Yes. Each deal has a Notes box.

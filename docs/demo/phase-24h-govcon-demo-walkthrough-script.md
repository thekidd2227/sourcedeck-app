# Phase 24H-PREP — GovCon Buyer Walkthrough Script (12–15 min)

**Date:** 2026-06-08
**Posture:** Docs / demo only. **No runtime change. No video. No screenshots.**
**Use with:** `phase-24h-buyer-demo-storyboard.md`, `phase-24h-demo-qa-checklist.md`.
**Pricing:** Only if the buyer asks — cite `docs/product/pricing-source-of-truth.md`. Prefer no pricing mention.

> Read the *intent*, not the words verbatim. Timings are targets. Hold the no-send/no-submit boundary throughout.

---

## 0. Opening — what SourceDeck is (≈1 min)

> "SourceDeck is a GovCon Capture OS — a desktop workspace that takes a government contractor from opportunity discovery all the way to a review-ready internal pursuit package, with a human approving every decision. Everything you'll see is sample demo data on my machine. One thing to set up front: SourceDeck helps me **organize, draft, and review**. It does **not** send email, submit bids, or upload to portals — I do those myself, outside the app."

---

## 1. The problem (≈1 min)

> "GovCon teams lose time moving from a SAM.gov discovery to a review-ready package — shredding the solicitation, building a compliance matrix, lining up vendors, pulling past performance, drafting a capability statement, mapping stakeholders, and checking readiness. It's scattered across spreadsheets and docs. SourceDeck puts that whole capture workflow in one place — and keeps a human in the loop the entire way."

---

## 2. Workflow demo (≈9–11 min)

### 2.1 Target profile / NAICS context (≈45s)
> "Targeting reads from my Pursuit Profile and NAICS — it's not a hard-coded list. That same profile drives the SAM Sprint scope and agency targeting." Show the profile-driven NAICS filter and Agency Targeting.

### 2.2 Capture Command Center (≈1 min)
> "Here's every active pursuit and its state — active, deadlines, Q&A, bid/no-bid, solicitation-ready, vendors, proposals, and what's waiting on my approval. Bid/no-bid is advisory — I decide." Show the Command Center counts and the advisory.

### 2.3 Operating Rhythm + deadlines (≈1 min)
> "This is my daily rhythm — the next moves across pursuits — and the deadline and Q&A calendar so I never miss a cutoff. Dates are operator-entered; I verify them against the live posting." Show Daily Rhythm and the Deadline & Q&A Calendar.

### 2.4 Solicitation + compliance workspace (≈1.5 min)
> "I shred the solicitation into Section L, M, B, C, H, K, the PWS, forms, deadlines, and risks. Then the compliance matrix gives every requirement a row — I mark each one reviewed; it defaults to Draft, never 'done' on its own." Show the Solicitation Workspace and Compliance Matrix.

### 2.5 Vendor + pricing workflow (≈1.5 min)
> "I line up subcontractor candidates in the Vendor Quote Room and track quote status — 'requested manually' means I reached out myself; SourceDeck doesn't send that outreach. The Pricing Worksheet gives advisory price and margin with warning bands for thin or missing inputs. I decide the number, and nothing here is submitted." Show Vendor Quote Room and Pricing Worksheet.

### 2.6 Past performance + capability workflow (≈1.5 min)
> "My Past Performance Library — projects, agency, NAICS, period, relevance, CPARS notes — reused and tailored per opportunity. And the Capability Statement Studio drafts a tailored statement; I can even paste existing text and it parses on-device into candidate fields — nothing is uploaded. The footer is explicit: internal review draft; SourceDeck does not send, submit, upload, or certify this content." Show Past Performance Library and Capability Statement Studio (with the local import).

### 2.7 Prime + stakeholder workflow (≈1.5 min)
> "Prime Partner Finder tracks candidate primes and teaming angle — 'contacted manually' again means I did it outside the app. The Stakeholder Graph shows who's around the opportunity — agency, program office, incumbent, prime/sub, my internal owner — with FAR-aware posture labels and a warning when we're in a restricted communication window. It's a reference. It never contacts anyone. Internal capture planning only — SourceDeck does not contact government officials, submit materials, upload to portals, or guarantee outcomes." Show Prime Partner Finder and the Stakeholder Graph (posture labels + restricted-window warning).

### 2.8 Submission readiness (≈45s)
> "The Submission Readiness Gate scores how ready the package is — red, yellow, green — and tells me what's missing. It's advisory and it never submits anything. There's a 'Human Review Required' notice right on it." Show the Submission Readiness Gate.

### 2.9 Internal review export (≈45s)
> "Finally I export the pursuit as an internal-review Markdown package — locally. The header says exactly what it is: 'INTERNAL REVIEW DRAFT — NOT SUBMITTED', and 'SourceDeck does not submit, upload, email, or transmit this package.' And every meaningful action is in the local audit log — my own evidence trail." Show the Internal Review Export preview and the Audit Log.

## 3. Boundary statement (≈30s)

> "To be completely clear: **SourceDeck prepares internal review materials. The user decides, approves, sends, uploads, and submits outside SourceDeck.** There's no Send Email button, no Submit Bid, no Submit Quote, no portal upload. It doesn't promise you an award, and it doesn't claim any compliance certification."

## 4. Close — paid pilot / request access (≈30s)

> "If this fits how you run capture, the next step is a guided pilot with your own Pursuit Profile and NAICS. You'd run a real opportunity end-to-end — discovery, qualification, compliance, vendors, past performance, capability statement, stakeholders, readiness, and the internal review package — and at every step you stay in control. Want to set that up?"

## Hard rules for the presenter

- Do **not** say SourceDeck wins contracts.
- Do **not** say SourceDeck submits bids / quotes / proposals.
- Do **not** say SourceDeck sends emails.
- Do **not** say SourceDeck uploads to portals.
- Do **not** say guaranteed award / revenue.
- Do **not** say certified FedRAMP / SOC 2 / CMMC / HIPAA / HITRUST / ISO.
- Do **not** say Apple notarized / production signed unless an exact release-evidence artifact proves it (current builds are unsigned dev builds).
- Keep any pricing aligned to `docs/product/pricing-source-of-truth.md`; prefer no pricing mention unless the buyer asks.
- If a buyer pushes a forbidden expectation ("does it submit my bid?", "is it FedRAMP certified?"), do **not** nod — restate the boundary and continue.
